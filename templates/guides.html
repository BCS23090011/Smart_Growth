Water Spinach (Kangkung)

Fast-growing leafy vegetable perfect for beginners

Difficulty: Very Easy

üå± Getting Started

1

Choose Your Seeds

Buy kangkung seeds from any garden store. Look for fresh, plump seeds. You can also use kangkung cuttings from the market - just make sure they have roots!

2

Prepare Your Container

Use a pot at least 6 inches deep with drainage holes. You can even use old plastic containers - just poke holes in the bottom for water to drain out.

3

Add Soil

Fill your container with regular potting soil. Don't worry about special soil - kangkung grows in almost anything! Mix in some compost if you have it.

üíß Planting & Watering

1

Plant Your Seeds

Make small holes about 1/2 inch deep. Drop 2-3 seeds in each hole and cover lightly with soil. Space holes about 2 inches apart.

2

Water Gently

Water the soil until it's moist but not soaking wet. Kangkung loves water, so keep the soil consistently damp. Water every day or when the top feels dry.

üåû Care & Harvesting

1

Provide Light

Place your pot where it gets morning sunlight. 4-6 hours of sun is perfect. Too much hot afternoon sun can make the leaves bitter.

2

Harvest Time!

In just 3-4 weeks, your kangkung will be ready! Cut the stems about 2 inches from the soil. The plant will grow back for more harvests!

üí° Beginner Tips

  • Kangkung grows super fast - you'll see sprouts in just 3-5 days!
  • If leaves turn yellow, you're probably overwatering. Let the soil dry out a bit.
  • You can grow kangkung in water too! Just put cuttings in a glass of water and change it every few days.
  • Harvest in the morning when leaves are crisp and fresh.

Okra (Lady's Finger)

Heat-loving vegetable that produces pods throughout the season

Difficulty: Easy

üå± Getting Started

1

Prepare Seeds

Soak okra seeds in warm water overnight. This helps them sprout faster. Choose a sunny spot - okra loves heat and sunshine!

2

Choose Container

Use a large pot (at least 12 inches deep) or plant directly in the ground. Okra plants can grow quite tall, so give them space!

3

Prepare Soil

Mix regular garden soil with some compost. Okra isn't picky about soil, but it should drain well. Avoid soil that stays soggy.

üåø Planting & Growing

1

Plant Seeds

Plant seeds 1 inch deep and 6 inches apart. If using pots, plant 1-2 seeds per large pot. Water gently after planting.

2

Wait for Sprouting

Seeds will sprout in 7-14 days. Keep soil moist but not waterlogged. Once plants are 4 inches tall, water deeply but less frequently.

3

Support Growing Plants

As plants grow taller (they can reach 4-6 feet!), you might need to stake them. Use a bamboo stick or wooden stake for support.

ü•í Harvesting

1

Watch for Pods

After 8-10 weeks, you'll see beautiful yellow flowers, followed by green pods. Harvest pods when they're 3-4 inches long and still tender.

2

Harvest Regularly

Pick pods every 2-3 days. The more you harvest, the more the plant will produce! Use scissors or a sharp knife to cut the stem.

üí° Beginner Tips

  • Okra loves hot weather - plant after the last frost in your area.
  • Don't harvest pods when they're wet - wait for them to dry to avoid spreading diseases.
  • If pods get too big and tough, leave them on the plant to save seeds for next season!
  • Wear gloves when harvesting - some people find okra plants a bit itchy.

Sweet Potato (Ubi Keledek)

Nutritious root vegetable that's surprisingly easy to grow

Difficulty: Easy

üç† Getting Started

1

Get Sweet Potato Slips

Buy sweet potato slips (baby plants) from a garden center, or grow your own by placing a sweet potato in water until it sprouts shoots.

2

Choose Your Space

Sweet potatoes need room to spread! Use a large container (at least 20 gallons) or plant in the ground with 3 feet between plants.

3

Prepare Loose Soil

Sweet potatoes grow best in loose, well-draining soil. Mix garden soil with compost and sand to make it fluffy - this helps the roots grow big!

üåø Planting & Care

1

Plant the Slips

Plant slips deep, burying 2/3 of the stem. Only the top leaves should show above soil. Water well after planting and keep soil moist for the first week.

2

Let Them Spread

Sweet potato vines will spread and cover the ground - this is normal! The vines will root at nodes, creating more sweet potatoes underground.

3

Water Wisely

After the first week, water only when the top inch of soil feels dry. Too much water can cause the roots to rot instead of growing sweet potatoes!

ü•î Harvesting

1

Wait Patiently

Sweet potatoes take 3-4 months to mature. You'll know they're ready when the vines start to yellow, or just before the first frost.

2

Dig Carefully

Use your hands or a small shovel to gently dig around the main plant. Sweet potatoes can be anywhere within 2 feet of the original plant!

3

Cure Before Eating

Let freshly dug sweet potatoes sit in a warm, humid place for a week. This makes them sweeter and helps them store longer!

üí° Beginner Tips

  • Don't worry if your sweet potatoes are oddly shaped - they'll still taste great!
  • You can eat the leaves too! Young sweet potato leaves are delicious in stir-fries.
  • Save some small sweet potatoes to grow slips for next year's crop.
  • Sweet potatoes can store for months in a cool, dark place - perfect for winter meals!
